Exercise: The Strength vs. Weakness Test

Open claude.ai (the free tier works) and try these two things. This is the real learning — doing it yourself.

Step 1: Give Claude something it's good at

Pick one of these and try it for real:

  • Ask it to help you think through a real decision you're facing
  • Paste a paragraph of your own writing and ask it to make it clearer
  • Ask it to explain something you've been meaning to learn

Notice: How is the response structured? Is it actually useful?

Step 2: Give Claude something it's bad at

Now try one of these:

  • Ask about something that happened this week in the news
  • Ask a very specific fact about a niche topic you know well
  • Ask it to predict what will happen next in your industry

Notice: Does it admit it doesn't know? Or does it just make something up?

Step 3: Reflect

Ask yourself:

  • What was the good-at task? How useful was it (1-5)?
  • What was the bad-at task? Did Claude own its limitation or fake it?
  • What surprised you?

This exercise builds the most important AI skill: calibration — knowing when to trust the output and when to double-check.
