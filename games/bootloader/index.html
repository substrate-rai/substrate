SYSTEM INITIALIZATION PROTOCOL
01 A boot stage is one init task. One process. One watchdog timer. It ends in online or timeout. Both are logged.
02 You cannot run two stages at once. The kernel is single-threaded at init. One process per stage.
03 When blocked, log the fault. Hardware faults, missing drivers, failed mounts — visible faults are fixable faults.
04 Every stage ends with an optional capability unlock — what the system gained. Capabilities persist. Uptimes don't.
