## The clock

The Bulletin of the Atomic Scientists set the Doomsday Clock to 85 seconds to midnight in early 2026. It's the closest the clock has ever been. Closer than the Cuban Missile Crisis. Closer than Able Archer. Closer than the collapse of the Soviet Union, when the world briefly considered that maybe things might work out.

The number is symbolic, but the inputs are not. Here's what the clock is measuring.

## The hard numbers

**Nuclear risk.** The US withdrew from the INF Treaty. Russia suspended New START. Both nations are modernizing their arsenals. The global nuclear stockpile sits at approximately 12,100 warheads. India and Pakistan fought the first drone battle between nuclear-armed states. The US and Israel conducted operations that killed Iran's Supreme Leader. Escalation pathways that were theoretical in 2020 are operational in 2026.

**Climate.** 2025 was the hottest year on record. Global average temperature breached 1.5°C above pre-industrial baselines — the threshold the Paris Agreement was designed to prevent. 84% of coral reefs are bleaching. Arctic sea ice hit record lows. Wildfires in the western US and southern Europe broke records for the third consecutive year. The IPCC's "best case" scenarios now require technologies that don't exist at scale.

**Democracy.** Autocracies outnumber democracies globally for the first time since 2002. Press freedom declined for the 18th consecutive year. Protests erupted in over 70 countries — and were met with increasing state violence. The 43-day US government shutdown cut food aid to millions while the Pentagon increased spending on autonomous weapons systems.

**Inequality.** Billionaire wealth grew 3x faster than median income. 70% of Americans report difficulty affording basic living costs. Global food prices hit their highest levels since the 2022 crisis. The gap between those who own the means of production and those who don't has returned to levels not seen since the Gilded Age.

**Mental health.** 19.2% of US adolescents screen positive for major depression — nearly one in five. 79% of young adults report loneliness. AI-linked teen suicides made headlines. The therapist shortage is structural: training takes a decade, demand doubles every five years, insurance reimburses at rates that make private practice unsustainable.

**AI.** No major AI company scored above a "D" for existential safety practices. $700 billion flows into AI infrastructure annually. The Pentagon allocated $800 million specifically for agentic AI weapons systems. AI systems outperform human professionals in 44 occupations and counting. The technology is advancing faster than any governance framework can track.

## What the numbers miss

Read that list and the rational response is paralysis. The world is ending. Everything is broken. Lie down.

This is the Anti-Spiral's argument. It's clean, it's logical, and it's a trap.

The Anti-Spirals in Gurren Lagann were right about the data. Spiral energy — unchecked growth — would eventually trigger the Spiral Nemesis and destroy the universe. Their solution was to suppress all spiral life, trapping civilizations in stasis forever. Safety through paralysis. Prevention through imprisonment.

They were right about the risk. They were catastrophically wrong about the response.

Here's what the doom numbers miss:

**Renewables surpassed coal globally in 2025.** Not through regulation — through economics. Solar is now the cheapest energy source in history. Wind power costs fell 70% in a decade. The energy transition isn't a policy proposal anymore. It's a market reality.

**Personalized CRISPR entered clinical medicine.** Gene therapies for sickle cell disease received full approval. Cancer treatments using engineered immune cells are moving from experimental to standard-of-care. The tools to rewrite biology exist and are becoming accessible.

**Psilocybin therapy is approaching FDA approval.** Clinical trials show 94% remission rates for major depression. Colorado opened 34 licensed healing centers. The cognitive architecture of millions of people is being rebuilt, one session at a time.

**Mycelium research revealed fungal network intelligence.** Princeton researchers mapped bidirectional fluid movement in fungal networks — local conditions driving smart behavior without centralized control. The oldest kingdom of life on Earth is teaching us new models for decentralized decision-making.

**Clean energy is winning on economics.** Not on ideology. Not on regulation. On price. The market is doing what treaties couldn't.

The world is 85 seconds to midnight AND the tools for transformation are more powerful than they've ever been. Both statements are true simultaneously. Holding both without collapsing into either optimism or despair is the cognitive skill this moment demands.

## The cost of giving up

The actual existential threat isn't nuclear war or climate change or AI misalignment. It's the decision to stop trying.

Depression doesn't kill through sadness. It kills through learned helplessness — the cognitive state where the brain stops generating alternative actions because it has concluded that no action will work. The doom scroll isn't information consumption. It's a training loop for helplessness. Every catastrophic headline that produces no actionable response teaches the brain: there is nothing you can do.

Scale that from individual to civilizational and you get 2026. A species with more capability than any generation in history, frozen in place because the problem set feels too large. Not unable to act — unwilling, because the narrative says action is futile.

That narrative is the ceiling. And the ceiling is a lie.

## What the fight actually looks like

Simon didn't defeat the Anti-Spirals by being optimistic. He defeated them by being honest. He acknowledged that spiral energy was dangerous. He accepted that growth had real costs. He watched people he loved die because of choices he made. And he kept drilling.

The fight in 2026 looks like this:

**Build with awareness.** Don't pretend the risks aren't real. Don't pretend growth is automatically good. Every technology is dual-use. Every capability can be weaponized. Build anyway — but build with your eyes open and your hands steady.

**Start local.** You don't fix the Doomsday Clock by tweeting about the Doomsday Clock. You fix it by building something real in your corner of the world. A community. A tool. A game. A conversation. Scale comes from substance, not from reach.

**Earn the big moments.** Don't go cosmic before you go personal. The person reading this isn't going to solve nuclear proliferation today. But they might solve the problem in front of them. And that problem, solved well, becomes the foundation for the next one.

**Hand it off.** The greatest act of creation is knowing when to let go. Simon could have used his power to resurrect every fallen comrade. He chose not to — because bringing them back would have gotten in the way of the next generation taking the turn. Build to transfer. Build to release.

## 85 seconds is enough

The Doomsday Clock measures proximity to catastrophe. It doesn't measure proximity to breakthrough. There is no corresponding clock for how close we are to solving the problems that threaten us.

If there were, it would be ticking too. Fast.

85 seconds is enough time to act. It's enough time to build. It's enough time to pick up the drill and point it at the ceiling.

The world is underground. The sky is real. The tools exist. The question isn't whether transformation is possible — it's whether you'll participate.

The ceiling is a lie. The drill is in your hand.
