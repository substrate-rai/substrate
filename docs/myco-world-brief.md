# Myco World — Project Brief

## Mission

Give people without pedigree a structured path to Claude fluency. Not just tool tutorials — opinionated guidance on what to learn, in what order, and why it matters for their specific goals.

## Why this exists

Anthropic has a free academy (Skilljar) that teaches Claude products — API, Claude Code, MCP, prompt engineering. It's organized by product, not by outcome. A layperson staring at 13 courses has no idea which ones matter for what they're trying to do, no sense of sequence, and no community to learn with.

Myco World fills the gap between "I've heard of Claude" and "I can use Claude to meaningfully change my work or career."

## Who it's for

Two audiences, one shared foundation:

**Non-technical practitioners** — real estate operators, MSP owners, small business people. They need Claude as a power tool for their existing work. They don't need to write code. They need to think clearly about what Claude can and can't do, prompt effectively, and build workflows.

**Non-pedigreed technical people** — IT directors, self-taught developers, career changers. They want to build on Claude and potentially work in AI. They have the capacity to learn the technical stack but lack the credential-based on-ramp that CS grads get by default.

Both groups share a core problem: nobody is giving them an opinionated path. Anthropic teaches features. Universities teach theory. Nobody says "you're here, you want to get there, do these things in this order."

## Structure (working model)

**Shared foundation (everyone):**
- What Claude actually is and isn't
- How to think with AI (decomposing problems, evaluating outputs, iterating systematically)
- Prompt craft as a real skill, not trial-and-error
- Understanding safety, limitations, and when not to use Claude
- Critical evaluation of AI outputs

**Path A — Practitioner (no code):**
- Claude.ai power usage
- Claude Code and Cowork as workflow tools
- Building automations without writing code
- Industry-specific applications
- Endpoint: dangerous in your own domain

**Path B — Builder (technical):**
- API and SDK fundamentals
- Tool use, structured outputs, MCP
- Agents, evals, orchestration
- Portfolio projects that demonstrate thinking, not just tool usage
- Safety and alignment literacy
- Endpoint: competitive for AI-adjacent or AI-native roles

## Format

Online community (platform TBD — Discord, Circle, custom, etc.). Not a solo self-paced course. Social accountability is the primary retention driver in gamified learning (Habitica's core lesson). People learning together, with structure to keep them moving.

## Open questions

1. **Platform**: Discord (free, familiar, but noisy) vs. Circle (cleaner, paid) vs. something else?
2. **Monetization**: Free, paid, freemium? Is this a product, a lead magnet for consulting, or a community-first play?
3. **Content cadence**: Cohort-based (everyone starts together), self-paced with community, or live sessions?
4. **Your role**: Founder/moderator, instructor, curator? How much of your time does this require weekly?
5. **First 10 members**: Who are they? People you know, or strangers from a specific audience?
6. **Relationship to Anthropic Academy**: Complement it (guide people through their free content with added structure) or replace it (build original content)?
7. **Competitive landscape**: What other AI learning communities exist and what do they get wrong?

## Evolution

The product pivoted three times: from "gamified API course" → "career path for Anthropic job seekers" → "Claude fluency community for non-pedigreed people." Each pivot was driven by asking "why" one more time. The final version solves a real problem (no structured path exists) for a real audience in a way that Anthropic's own offering doesn't.
